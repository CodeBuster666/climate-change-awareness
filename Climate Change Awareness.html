What is Climate Changes?

 Climate change refers to significant changes in average weather conditions—such as becoming warmer, wetter, or drier—over several decades or more. This long-term trend distinguishes climate change from natural weather variability. Its effects are diverse, including record floods, raging storms, and deadly heat, impacting all living beings, but not equally. Economically disadvantaged communities and people of color, who contribute the least to its causes, suffer the most from its impacts. The basics include understanding the causes of climate change, its effects on the planet and people, and potential solutions.

What is Climate Change and Global Heating?
How Does it Work to Affect Our Lives?

Climate change, or global warming, results from Earth's average temperature increase due to rising greenhouse gas concentrations and human-induced emissions. Greenhouse gases such as water vapor, carbon dioxide, methane, nitrous oxide, and ozone trap heat radiation in the atmosphere, intensifying warming by absorbing and emitting heat. This phenomenon alters global climate patterns, driven primarily by human activities, with scientific consensus attributing over 95% of observed warming to these factors.

Integrating volunteerism in climate change adaptation: PNVSCA and CCC explore areas of collaboration

On 14 February 2023, the PNVSCA and CCC met to integrate volunteerism into climate resilience initiatives in the Philippines. They discussed combining volunteerism advocacy with climate change awareness during Earth Month and CCC Week. PNVSCA proposed Volunteer Management training to enhance volunteer engagement.

Youth in Climate Action - #NowPH

In response to the Manila Call to Action on Climate Change, the National Youth Commission and Climate Change Commission launched the #NowPH campaign. This campaign urges youth to help prevent global warming from exceeding two degrees Celsius. It involves various agencies and notable Filipino figures.

Plastic Ban And Regulation

Indulge The Cabinet Cluster on Climate Change Adaptation approved Resolution No. 1 to phase out single-use plastics and promote sustainable products. This includes the Philippine Action Plan for Sustainable Consumption, led by NEDA, and establishes a group to support an Anti-Single Use Plastics Law. Additionally, the Climate Change Commission has banned plastics in its office, and the House Committee on Ecology approved House Bill 9147 to regulate single-use plastics.

Communities For Resilience

Efforts to defeat poverty and promote social justice require helping vulnerable communities adapt to climate change. Studies show climate changes will impact agriculture, water resources, health, and urban areas, affecting food and water security and environmental stability. In response, the Climate Change Commission implemented the CORE Capacity Building Training Program, aligning with national and global frameworks.

Catbalogan Sky City Mega Project

Catbalogan's Sky City, located 120 meters above sea level on 440 hectares of land, addresses the city's vulnerabilities to landslides, storms, and floods while combating congestion. Designed for learning, working, playing, and resting, Sky City promotes walking and biking to reduce emissions, powered entirely by renewable energy sources. Each household will have a garden, aiming to set a sustainability model for the country and beyond, with potential regional and national impact as a climate-resilient urban center and evacuation hub.
